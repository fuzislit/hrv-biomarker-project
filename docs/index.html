Overview

Why this project matters

Rare neurological diseases like ALS and Huntington's are notoriously difficult to study — there simply isn't enough patient data to train robust models. We found a different path: build a normative baseline from healthy individuals, and use prediction error as the signal for disease.

⚡

The problem

Rare disease datasets are scarce. Traditional supervised ML approaches fail when you have fewer than a few dozen patients with labeled progression data.

🧬

Our approach

Train exclusively on healthy ECG data to learn what "normal" looks like. When unhealthy data is fed in, the model's prediction errors flag disease progression.

📊

Synthetic data

We generate synthetic HRV signals that closely mirror real healthy patient patterns — expanding the training pool without requiring more patients.

🎯

Target disorders

Any rare neurological disorder — including ALS, Huntington's Disease, and Muscular Dystrophy — where early biomarker detection could transform patient outcomes.

Methodology

How it works

A four-stage pipeline from raw ECG signal to synthetic HRV generation.

01

Extract RR intervals

RR interval data is extracted from ECG signals of 54 healthy individuals using the NSR2DB dataset — digitized at 128 Hz with manual beat annotation review.

02

Train CNN and LSTM models

Both a Convolutional Neural Network and Long Short-Term Memory model are trained to predict the next heartbeat interval from a sliding window of 50 prior RR intervals.

03

Cross-validate to reduce overfitting

Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation (LOOCV) and K-Fold validation ensure the models generalize across patients, not just the training set.

04

Generate synthetic HRV data

The trained models generate synthetic HRV sequences that closely mirror real patient patterns — establishing the healthy baseline used for future disease detection.

Dataset

Normal Sinus Rhythm RR Interval Database

All models were trained exclusively on healthy patient data from the NSR2DB — a gold-standard dataset for cardiac rhythm research.

54 long-term ECG recordings from healthy individuals
128 Hz digitization rate with manual annotation review
50 RR interval sliding window for prediction
Source: NSR2DB (Normal Sinus Rhythm RR Interval Database)  ·  Healthy adults only  ·  ECG beat annotations manually reviewed for accuracy
